The course library

Materials

The course can be followed freely online. A notebook and pen are the only essential physical materials; printed books are helpful, but they are not required.

Main course text

Joseph Deharbe — A Complete Catechism of the Catholic Religion

John Fander translation · sixth American edition · edited by James J. Fox and Thomas McMillan · 1912

This is the course spine. Its orderly questions and explanations provide the historical route through Catholic doctrine. Each lesson then checks and develops that reading through Scripture, the modern Catechism and other authoritative sources.

Read online Open Library edition Find a print copy Search libraries

Use the lesson’s article, section and question references rather than page numbers, because pagination varies between editions and reprints.

A sensible approach: begin with the free online text. Buy a physical copy only if you find that annotation, longer reading sessions or reduced screen use would genuinely improve your study. For scarce books, try your local public library’s reservation or inter-library loan service before purchasing.

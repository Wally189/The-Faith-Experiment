Course one · first draft

Foundations of the Catholic faith

Twelve connected investigations rather than twelve disconnected talks.

Course question: What has God revealed, how does the Church know it, and what difference should it make to an ordinary life?
Lesson 1

The God question

Why human beings ask about God, what reason can discover, and where its limits lie.
Lesson 2

What revelation is

God reveals himself—not merely a collection of religious facts.

Lesson 3

Scripture and Tradition

Why Catholics refuse both a Bible without the Church and a tradition detached from Scripture.

Lesson 4

Who is Jesus?

The historical question, the Gospel portraits and the confession of true God and true man.

Lesson 5

Cross and Resurrection

What Christians mean by salvation—and what they do not mean.

Lesson 6

Grace and freedom

Gift, response, justification, merit and the false choice between God’s action and ours.

Lesson 7

Why matter matters

Incarnation, signs and the sacramental imagination.

Lesson 8

The Eucharistic centre

Passover, sacrifice, presence, communion and mission.

Lesson 9

Conscience and virtue

Why Catholic morality is more than rule-keeping or private sincerity.

Lesson 10

Learning to pray

Prayer as relationship, discipline and transformation.

Lesson 11

The common good

Dignity, solidarity, subsidiarity and Catholic social doctrine in British and Irish life.

Lesson 12

The lay apostolate

How an educated faith becomes service rather than private religious collecting.

The course is a guided programme of independent lay formation, not a seminary, degree or substitute for ecclesial oversight.

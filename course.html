A public experiment in Catholic self-education

Foundations of the Catholic faith

A guided journey through Joseph Deharbe’s substantial catechism, read critically and faithfully alongside Scripture, the modern Catechism and current Church teaching.

The course spine is now decided: Deharbe supplies the historical route; the living teaching of the Church governs its interpretation.
Primary historical text

Joseph Deharbe

A serious older text worth recovering, but never treated as though every historical formulation or discipline were timeless.

Working edition

A Complete Catechism of the Catholic Religion

Translated by Rev. John Fander; sixth American edition; edited by Rev. James J. Fox and Rev. Thomas McMillan; New York, 1912.

Controlling sources

Old text, living Church

Sacred Scripture, the Catechism of the Catholic Church and relevant magisterial sources control interpretation.

Supporting texts

Memory and depth

The English Penny Catechism supports recall; the Roman Catechism, Aquinas and the Fathers provide selected deeper readings.

The method

How each lesson works

Encounter → read Deharbe → check and distinguish → test objections → write by hand → demonstrate understanding.

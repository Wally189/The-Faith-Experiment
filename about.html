About the project

The Faith Experiment

An independent lay project about reading Catholic texts carefully, understanding what they say and forming a considered view.

What is being tested?

The Church’s teaching is not the experiment. The experiment is whether an ordinary person can take a substantial Catholic text, read it attentively, check it against authoritative modern sources and explain what has been understood.

The present course follows Joseph Deharbe’s A Complete Catechism of the Catholic Religion. Deharbe supplies the route through the subject. Sacred Scripture, the Catechism of the Catholic Church and relevant magisterial sources supply the authoritative measure.

Who made it?

I’m Alan, a lay Catholic with an interest in Church teaching and in finding better ways to read serious books without merely passing my eyes over the page. I am not presenting myself as a priest, theologian or official teacher of the Church. The course records a method of study and invites others to follow it for themselves.

How it works

The basic equipment is deliberately ordinary: the assigned text, a notebook and a pen. Each lesson asks the reader to begin with their own thoughts, read the text, take structured notes, consult the modern Catechism and Scripture, test the argument and record what they now think.

The site is intended to guide study rather than keep the reader on a screen. Books may be obtained in print or read lawfully online.

Authority, neutrality and corrections

This is an independent website, not an official publication of the Catholic Church or of any diocese. It aims to present Catholic teaching accurately and neutrally, without factional argument or culture-war commentary.

The Catechism of the Catholic Church is the principal modern authoritative reference used throughout the course. Where an error is identified, it will be checked and corrected openly. Corrections and suggested sources can be sent through the contact page.

Read carefully, check honestly, and correct openly.

Why this exists

For Catholics who want an education, not merely more Catholic content.

The Faith Experiment asks whether an ordinary lay person can use freely available texts, disciplined study and trustworthy guidance to build a serious foundation in the faith.

Not a personality cult

The presenter is a learner showing the work, not a self-appointed guru pretending to possess every answer.

Not a factional project

Old sources are neither mocked for being old nor treated as binding merely because they are old.

Not a substitute

The project cannot replace parish life, sacramental formation, spiritual direction, safeguarding, accredited theology or ecclesiastical authority.

The cultural voice

The project is consciously British and Irish in tone and examples. It aims to make difficult material vivid through questions, evidence, objects, stories, comparisons and occasional surprises—without turning theology into entertainment or importing somebody else’s culture war.

Never make the past sound stupid merely because it is past; never make it binding merely because it is old; and never make something Catholic merely because a Catholic writer once said it.

The public experiment

Each course will leave behind a replicable website: clean primary text where lawful, guided lessons, notebook prompts, source links, corrections, videos and a final demonstration of learning. The essential route should remain free. Optional income may come from printed notebooks, annotated editions, guided cohorts and supporter material—not from placing Catholic doctrine behind a paywall.

An independent lay project in service of clearer, deeper Catholic formation.

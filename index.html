A public experiment in lay Catholic formation

Can an ordinary person build a serious Catholic education?

Begin with a substantial old book. Read it slowly. Write by hand. Check it against the living teaching of the Church. Then show what you have learned.

This website follows that experiment in public. It is neither an effortless online course nor a claim to replace formal theology. It is a guided route through Scripture, doctrine, history, philosophy and the spiritual life for adults who want an education—not merely more content.

The Experiment method

Old discipline. Modern guidance.

The screen provides the route; the book provides substance; the notebook creates memory; the video provides companionship.

1

Encounter

Begin with a human question, surprising object, historical dispute or apparent contradiction.

2

Read and write

Read a manageable primary passage, then summarise it by hand without copying its language.

3

Test and distinguish

Separate dogma, doctrine, discipline, opinion and historical circumstance.

4

Demonstrate

Explain, map, compare or apply the idea so that learning becomes visible.

Course one

Foundations of the Catholic faith

A first twelve-lesson path. It begins with revelation rather than isolated rules and ends with prayer and mission rather than a completion badge.

The primary text

The historical text supplies structure and precision. It is never treated as though age itself makes every sentence binding.

The controlling references

Scripture, the Catechism, Canon Law and relevant magisterial documents govern the interpretation.

The honest journal

Videos and written entries record first impressions, corrections, unresolved questions and genuine progress.

A PUBLIC EXPERIMENT IN CATHOLIC SELF-EDUCATION

Welcome to The Faith Experiment.

Working through Joseph Deharbe’s A Complete Catechism of the Catholic Religion with handwritten notes, honest objections and careful checking against Scripture and the living teaching of the Church.

The Church’s teaching is not the experiment. The experiment is whether an ordinary person can read it carefully, understand it properly and form a considered view of what the text is saying.

The course is written in general terms for Catholics, enquirers and anyone interested in understanding the Catholic faith. I’m a Catholic who has often struggled simply to read a serious book and properly absorb it. This site documents that study honestly and invites others to follow along with a book, notebook and pen. Schedule

The aim is to read slowly enough to remember. A lesson combines reading, handwritten recall, source checking and a short explanation in your own words.

How to study

Find somewhere reasonably comfortable and quiet, with your book, notebook and pen close at hand. Allow roughly 45 minutes to one hour for a lesson, but treat that as a guide rather than a rule.

Some lessons may be completed comfortably in one sitting. Others may be divided across two or three shorter sessions: reading first, notes and source-checking later, then a final review. There is no penalty for pausing, repeating a section or taking longer. The aim is understanding and retention, not keeping pace with an artificial timetable.

A sensible rhythm

A lesson may be completed in one sitting or divided across the week.

Session 1: starting thoughts and Deharbe reading

Session 2: guided notes, distinctions and questions

Session 3: source checking, review and demonstration

Course blocks

1. God and Revelation

Reason, desire, revelation, Scripture and Tradition.

2. Christ and Salvation

Incarnation, Cross, Resurrection, grace and redemption.

3. Sacraments and Christian Life

Worship, sacramental life, virtue and conscience.

4. Prayer and Mission

Prayer, holiness, social doctrine and lay vocation.

Certificates

The Faith Experiment certificate framework has not yet been released. It will be approved only after the course source, pilot lessons and completion test are settled.

Evidence of study

Completion will require active notebook work, source checking, recall and a final explanation in the learner’s own words.

Clear scope

Any future certificate will state exactly which course block was completed and what evidence was required.

Honest limits

It will not claim academic accreditation, professional qualification or ecclesiastical approval.

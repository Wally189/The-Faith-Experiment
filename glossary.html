Plain-English reference

Catholic terms

Brief explanations of terms used in the course. These are reading aids, not replacements for the full teaching of the Church.

Catechism

An organised presentation of Christian teaching, usually arranged for instruction. In this course, “the modern Catechism” means the Catechism of the Catholic Church, the principal modern authoritative reference for Catholic doctrine. Read it at the Vatican →

Divine revelation

God freely making himself and his saving purpose known. Catholics believe revelation reaches its fullness in Jesus Christ and is handed on in Sacred Scripture and Sacred Tradition.

Faith

The human response to God who reveals. It involves grace, intellect, will, trust and the whole person; it is not simply believing something without reason.

Magisterium

The Church’s teaching office, exercised by the Pope and the bishops in communion with him. It serves the Word of God by faithfully guarding, explaining and handing on the deposit of faith.

Natural knowledge of God

Knowledge that human reason can reach from creation and human experience, without first depending upon truths known only because God has revealed them.

Sacred Scripture

The books of the Bible received by the Church as inspired by God. Scripture is read within the living Tradition of the Church.

Sacred Tradition

The living transmission of the Gospel entrusted by Christ and the Holy Spirit to the apostles and handed on in the Church. It does not simply mean customs or old habits.

The glossary will grow only when a course term genuinely needs explanation.
